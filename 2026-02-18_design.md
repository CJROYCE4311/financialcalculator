# Design Document: Finance Web Application

**Date:** 2026-02-18
**Project:** Website Finance

## 1. Landing Page Design (Styling & Layout)

The landing page will follow a professional, high-trust financial services aesthetic inspired by Fisher Investments. The focus is on clarity, minimalism, and a conversion-oriented layout.

### Visual Identity
*   **Color Palette:**
    *   **Primary:** Deep Sherwood Green (#004A3D) or Navy Blue for headers and primary CTAs.
    *   **Secondary/Accent:** Mountain Meadow Teal (#15c18f) for interactive elements and highlights.
    *   **Background:** Clean White (#FFFFFF) with Light Grey (#F8F9FA) section backgrounds to create depth.
    *   **Typography:** 
        *   **Headers:** Elegant Serif font (e.g., Playfair Display or Georgia) to convey authority and stability.
        *   **Body:** Clean Sans-Serif font (e.g., Inter or Roboto) for high readability.
*   **Atmosphere:** Professional, stable, and transparent. Use of significant white space to avoid clutter.

### Layout Structure
*   **Hero Section:** A split-screen or centered hero with a high-quality, professional image and a clear value proposition.
*   **Navigation:** Minimalist header with links to specific calculators.
*   **Trust Indicators:** Clean sections for "How it works" or "Why use our tools."
*   **Call to Action (CTA):** Prominent buttons in accent colors.

## 2. Calculators

### 2.1 Investment Projection Calculator
This tool helps users visualize their wealth growth based on their current situation and future contributions.

*   **User Inputs:**
    *   **Current Balance:** Numeric input.
    *   **Investment Mix (Sliders):** Three linked sliders for Equities, Bonds, and Cash (Money Market). Total must equal 100%.
    *   **User Age:** Numeric input/slider.
    *   **Annual Investment:** How much the user will invest per year.
    *   **Employer Match:** Annual dollar amount or percentage.
    *   **Retirement Age:** Targeted age to stop contributing.
*   **Visual Outputs:**
    *   **Pie Chart:** Showing the current/target Investment Mix.
    *   **Area Chart:** Balance projection by year until retirement, showing growth based on projected returns for the selected mix.

### 2.2 Retirement Withdrawal Calculator
Uses the projected balance from the Investment Projection to estimate sustainable income.

*   **User Inputs:**
    *   **Projected Balance:** (Pre-filled from Calculator 1, but editable).
    *   **Starting Withdrawal Rate:** Percentage input (e.g., default 4%).
    *   **Inflation Adjustment:** Toggle or percentage to adjust annual withdrawals.
*   **Visual Outputs:**
    *   **Bar/Line Chart:** Graphically represent annual withdrawals over time, layered next to other retirement sources (Social Security, Pensions).

### 2.3 Defined Retirement (Pension/Annuity)
Input for fixed retirement income sources.

*   **User Inputs:**
    *   **Type Selection:** Lifetime vs. Fixed Term (e.g., 10 years).
    *   **Benefit Amount:** $X per month or year.
    *   **Starting Age:** When the benefit begins.

### 2.4 Social Security Estimator
Provides a baseline estimate of Social Security benefits.

*   **User Inputs:**
    *   **Final Salary:** Projected annual salary at retirement.
    *   **Retirement Age:** Age when benefits are claimed (62 to 70).
*   **Logic:** Estimate benefit based on typical SSA formulas (simplified for the web app).

### 2.5 Retirement Budget & Cash Flow Analysis
Analyzes the gap between projected income and desired lifestyle spending.

*   **User Inputs:**
    *   **Annual Budget:** Current dollar amount for desired retirement lifestyle.
    *   **Inflation Rate:** Expected annual inflation (default 3%).
*   **Logic:** 
    *   Project the budget forward based on the inflation rate.
    *   Compare total annual income (Calculators 2.2, 2.3, 2.4) against the projected budget.
*   **Visual Outputs:**
    *   **Stacked Area Chart:** Total income vs. Total expenses over time, highlighting "Surplus" vs. "Shortfall" years.

### 2.6 Monte Carlo Simulation Engine
A rigorous stress test of the retirement plan against market volatility.

*   **Execution:** 1,000,000 simulation instances.
*   **Parameters:** Uses historical volatility and mean returns based on the user's Investment Mix (Equities/Bonds/Cash).
*   **Logic:** Simulates 1,000,000 different market paths to see how often the portfolio survives the full retirement duration.
*   **Visual Outputs:**
    *   **Success Rate Gauge:** High-impact visual showing the percentage of successful trials (e.g., "92% Success Probability").
    *   **Probability Distribution:** A "Fan Chart" showing the range of possible outcomes from the 5th to 95th percentile.

### 2.7 Strategy Narrative Generator
An automated, expert-level report summarizing the financial outlook.

*   **Functionality:** Generates a ~500-word comprehensive narrative based on the specific results of all calculators and simulations.
*   **Content Pillars:**
    *   **Strategy Summary:** Analysis of the asset allocation and contribution habits.
    *   **Income Outlook:** Breakdown of how Social Security, Pensions, and Withdrawals interact.
    *   **Success Evaluation:** Interpretation of the Monte Carlo results (interpreting the risk of failure).
    *   **Recommendations:** Actionable steps to improve success rate (e.g., "Increase savings by 2%," "Delay Social Security to age 70," or "Reduce equity exposure").

*   **Responsive Design:** Seamless experience across desktop, tablet, and mobile.
*   **Tooltips:** Explain financial terms (e.g., "4% Rule," "Employer Match").
